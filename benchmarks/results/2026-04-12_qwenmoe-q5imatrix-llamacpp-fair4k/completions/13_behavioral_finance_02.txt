**The Disposition Effect Diagnosis**

Shefrin and Statman's disposition effect framework, synthesized by Marks and others, identifies a specific asymmetry in how traders process gains versus losses. The trader's data tells the complete story:

- Winners held 4.2 days on average → sold too early (taking profits prematurely)
- Losers held 11.7 days on average → held too long (avoiding realization of loss)
- 14 trades that ran an additional 15–30% after the trader took profits → this is the most damning evidence. These represent the opportunity cost of the disposition effect in action.

The prospect-theory utility function that drives this is convex for losses and concave for gains. When a trader is in the domain of gains (a winning position), the utility function is concave — the marginal utility of additional gains diminishes. This creates a risk-averse attitude toward further upside: "I've already made $420, why risk it?" The trader locks in the gain.

When a trader is in the domain of losses (a losing position), the utility function is convex — the marginal disutility of additional losses diminishes. This creates a risk-seeking attitude toward further downside: "If I sell now, I realize the loss. If I hold, maybe it comes back." The trader avoids realizing the loss.

**The Structured Exit Protocol**

The solution requires systematic rules that override the emotional impulse. Here is a concrete framework:

**Rule 1: Pre-Defined Exit Targets**
Before entering any trade, define three exit levels:
- Target 1 (conservative): 1.5R — take 40% of position off
- Target 2 (standard): 2.5R — take 40% of position off
- Target 3 (runner): trail stop to breakeven, let remaining 20% run

This forces the trader to commit to the exit plan before the emotional state of the trade develops.

**Rule 2: No Early Exit Without a Valid Signal**
The trader may only exit before Target 1 if one of these conditions is met:
- The original thesis is invalidated (fundamental or technical)
- A stop-loss level is hit
- The position size exceeds the maximum risk per trade (1–2% of portfolio)

Any other reason (fear, greed, boredom, desire to "lock in profits") is not a valid exit signal.

**Rule 3: The 5-Day Minimum Hold Rule for Winners**
For any trade that is in profit by at least 1R, the trader must hold for a minimum of 5 trading days before considering an early exit. This directly addresses the 4.2-day average holding time for winners — it forces the trader to stay in the winners long enough for the edge to express itself.

**Rule 4: The 2-Day Maximum Hold Rule for Losers**
For any trade that is in loss by at least 0.5R, the trader must review the position within 2 trading days. If the thesis is not confirmed by price action (the trade is moving in the expected direction), the trader must exit at the stop-loss level. This directly addresses the 11.7-day average holding time for losers.

**Rule 5: Weekly Review Using the Disposition Audit**
Each week, the trader reviews all closed trades and categorizes them:
- Sold winner early (before Target 1): count as disposition error
- Held loser past stop: count as disposition error
- Held winner to Target 2 or 3: count as correct behavior
- Held loser to stop: count as correct behavior

The goal is to reduce disposition errors from the current rate (approximately 14 missed runners + all the early winners) to below 20% of total trades.

**Rule 6: The Position Sizing Feedback Loop**
If the disposition audit shows more than 3 disposition errors in a week, the trader must reduce position size by 50% for the following week. This creates a direct financial consequence for the behavioral error.
