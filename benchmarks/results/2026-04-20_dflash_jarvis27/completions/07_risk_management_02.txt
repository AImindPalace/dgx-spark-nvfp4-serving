**Hierarchical Risk Parity Framework Application:**

DePrado's method allocates risk across three levels: (1) individual position risk, (2) portfolio-level risk (correlation-adjusted), and (3) fund-level risk (drawdown limits). The fund is currently violating the hierarchy because portfolio-level risk ($39.84M at 8.3% heat) exceeds fund-level risk capacity ($18.24M remaining to the 15% hard stop). The hierarchical framework demands that fund-level constraints override portfolio-level and position-level signals — this is the risk parity principle applied to the fund's survival.

**Volatility-Scaled Position Sizing:**

With ATR expanded 40% above the 20-day average, the volatility-targeting model requires position sizes to contract by 1/1.40 = 28.6%. Applied to the current 8.3% portfolio heat, the volatility-adjusted target is 8.3% × 0.714 = 5.93%. However, this is the model's normal-state target — the drawdown context requires further reduction.

**Two-Day Execution Plan:**

*Day 1:* Reduce portfolio heat from 8.3% to 5.0% by scaling all positions proportionally. This means selling approximately 40% of each position. Execution should use TWAP algorithms over 4-6 hours to minimize market impact. Priority: liquidate the most illiquid positions first (lowest daily volume, widest spreads) because their execution risk is highest in elevated volatility.

*Day 2:* Reassess realized volatility. If ATR has normalized (within 10% of 20-day average), restore heat to 6.5-7.0%. If ATR remains elevated or expands further, reduce heat to 3.5-4.0%. The decision rule: heat = min(7.0%, 8.3% × (ATR_20day / ATR_current)).

**Full Stop-Out Threshold:**

The fund should initiate a full stop-out when either: (1) drawdown reaches -13.5% (leaving 1.5% buffer above the 15% hard limit), or (2) portfolio heat exceeds 12% at any point. At -13.5% drawdown, remaining capacity is $7.2M, and any heat above 1.5% of NAV creates unacceptable breach risk. The stop-out should be executed via the same TWAP method — panic selling into elevated volatility destroys more value than the drawdown itself.

**Operational Risks of Liquidation in Elevated Volatility:**

Three specific risks: (1) Bid-ask spread widening — in a 40% ATR expansion, spreads on futures contracts typically widen 50-100%, adding $0.50-1.00 per contract in execution cost. On a $480M book, this could add $200K-500K in slippage. (2) Correlation spikes — during drawdowns, historically uncorrelated positions become correlated as risk-off selling hits everything simultaneously, meaning the portfolio-level risk is higher than the sum of individual position risks. (3) Forced selling creates a self-reinforcing loop — the fund's own liquidation contributes to the adverse price movement, increasing the drawdown further.

The behavioral dimension is critical: the risk committee's 15% hard limit is a form of the 'stop-loss' rule that Dalio's framework would classify as a structural constraint. The fund must respect it absolutely — no exceptions, no 'one more day' rationalizations. The -11.2% drawdown is already in the zone where loss aversion (Kahneman's framework) makes every additional loss feel disproportionately painful, which is precisely when disciplined execution matters most.
