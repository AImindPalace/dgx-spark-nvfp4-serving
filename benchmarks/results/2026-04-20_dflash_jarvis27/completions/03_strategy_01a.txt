The Kelly Criterion provides a mathematically optimal bet-sizing framework, but its practical application requires significant adjustment for real-world trading. Here's the complete analysis.

**Kelly Fraction Calculation**

Using the standard Kelly formula f* = (p × b - q) / b:
- p (win rate) = 0.58
- q (loss rate) = 0.42
- b (payoff ratio) = 4.2% / 2.8% = 1.5

f* = (0.58 × 1.5 - 0.42) / 1.5 = 0.30, or 30% of capital per trade.

At full Kelly on a $250,000 account: $75,000 per trade.

**Drawdown During Maximum Observed Loss Run**

With 9 consecutive losses at full Kelly, the compound drawdown is: $250,000 × (1 - 0.30 × 0.028)^9 = $250,000 × (0.9916)^9 = $231,825. That's a 7.3% drawdown from peak equity.

This looks manageable — but it's dangerously misleading for three reasons.

**Why Full-Kelly Is Impractical for Discretionary Traders**

1. **Parameter Estimation Error**: The 58% win rate, 4.2% average win, and 2.8% average loss are estimates from approximately 750–1,250 trades over 5 years. If the true win rate is 54% (a plausible 4-percentage-point estimation error), the true Kelly fraction is only 21.3%. Betting at 30% when the true Kelly is 21.3% means you're overbetting, which produces negative geometric growth over time. The Kelly framework is maximally sensitive to parameter error — overbetting is worse than underbetting because it can lead to ruin.

2. **Observed Maximum ≠ True Maximum**: The 9 consecutive losses observed in 5 years is a sample statistic. The probability of 15 consecutive losses with a 42% loss rate is (0.42)^15 ≈ 2.3 × 10^-8 — rare but not impossible over a 20-year career. At full Kelly, 15 consecutive losses produce a 12.0% drawdown. At 20 consecutive losses: 16.1%. The tail risk of longer-than-observed loss runs is the primary danger.

3. **Discretionary Execution Variance**: The backtest assumes consistent execution. A discretionary trader introduces behavioral variance — hesitation on entries, premature exits, emotional position sizing adjustments. This execution noise further degrades the edge, making the estimated parameters even less reliable.

**Practical Recommendation: Half-Kelly**

At half-Kelly (15% of capital = $37,500 per trade), the 9-loss drawdown shrinks to approximately 3.7%, and the strategy tolerates parameter estimation error of up to 3 percentage points in win rate before the Kelly fraction turns negative. The geometric growth rate at half-Kelly is approximately 75% of the maximum achievable at full Kelly — a small sacrifice for dramatically improved robustness.
