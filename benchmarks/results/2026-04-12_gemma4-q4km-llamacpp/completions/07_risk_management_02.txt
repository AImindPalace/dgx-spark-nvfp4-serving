The core principle is that position sizing should be inversely proportional to volatility, ensuring each position contributes a roughly equal risk contribution to the portfolio. When the flagship S&P position's ATR has expanded 40% above its 20-day average, the volatility-targeting model must reduce that position's size by approximately 28.6% (1 - 1/1.40 = 0.286) to maintain constant risk contribution. **Operational Risks of Liquidating into Elevated Volatility:** This is the most critical practical concern. The fund's volatility-targeting model calls for reducing positions precisely when market impact costs are highest. The 40% ATR expansion means bid-ask spreads have widened, and the fund's own liquidation could contribute to further price deterioration. The risk committee should also consider the possibility that the volatility spike is a false signal — a single-day spike that mean-reverts — and that reducing positions now locks in a worse entry for the next re-entry. The operational risk is that the fund's systematic rules create a feedback loop: volatility triggers position reduction, which in a thin market contributes to further volatility.
