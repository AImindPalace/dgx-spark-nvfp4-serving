This scenario requires applying Aronson's Bayesian framework to a discretionary trading decision — a direct application of the chapter's core thesis that disciplined traders should update their beliefs in light of new evidence rather than clinging to a prior thesis. The trader's original thesis had three components, each with its own probability. Let's formalize the prior:

**Prior Beliefs (Pre-NFP):**
- US growth decelerates: 65% probability (this was the core thesis driver)
- Fed cuts 3x: 45% probability (conditional on growth decelerating)
- ECB holds longer: 55% probability (independent of US data)
- Combined probability of full thesis playing out: 0.65 × 0.45 × 0.55 = 16%

**New Evidence:**
- NFP +312,000 vs. +175,000 consensus: +78% surprise
- Average hourly earnings +4.1% YoY: accelerating wage growth
- GDPNow 3.4%: above consensus, above trend
- Fed futures: 1.2 cuts vs. 3 expected

**Bayesian Update:**
The NFP surprise is the most directly relevant data point. Using a simple Bayesian update: if the original thesis was that growth would decelerate, the probability of seeing +312,000 jobs when you expected +175,000 is low — let's estimate P(data|thesis) ≈ 0.15. The probability under the null hypothesis (growth is NOT decelerating) is higher — let's estimate P(data|null) ≈ 0.40. Posterior probability of deceleration given this data = (0.15 × 0.65) / [(0.15 × 0.65) + (0.40 × 0.35)] = 0.0975 / (0.0975 + 0.14) = 41.2%.

The growth deceleration probability has dropped from 65% to 41.2%. The Fed cuts probability, conditional on growth, drops from 45% to roughly 30% (since the conditional probability is now 30/41.2 × 41.2% = 30%). The ECB component remains unchanged at 55%. Combined posterior: 0.412 × 0.30 × 0.55 = 6.8%.

The thesis has been downgraded from 16% to 6.8% — a 60% reduction in probability.

**Thesis Invalidation vs. Noise:**
This is the critical distinction. The NFP print is a single data point — it could be a one-month outlier (noise). However, the wage data (+4.1% YoY) and GDPNow revision (3.4%) provide independent confirmation that the growth deceleration thesis is facing headwinds. This isn't noise — it's a coherent data cluster. The thesis is NOT invalidated (6.8% is not zero), but it is severely weakened.

**Actionable Decision:**
The position is currently at 1.0940, +120 pips from entry at 1.0820. The trader should reduce the position by 50% immediately. The remaining 50% should have a stop at 1.0880 (40 pips above entry, below the recent swing low) and a revised target of 1.0750 (the original target, now with lower probability). The trader should also reduce the position size further if the next two months of data confirm the growth acceleration. The thesis survives at reduced size until the probability drops below 5% — at which point the position should be closed entirely.
