Excellent question. This is a classic case of how disciplined discretionary macro trading operates at the intersection of theory, data, and risk management. Let's walk through the Bayesian update process as inspired by Aronson's methodology, focusing on the *process* of belief revision.

### **1. Formal Bayesian Update of the Prior Thesis**

The trader's initial thesis rests on three interconnected pillars:
1.  **P1:** US growth decelerates.
2.  **P2:** Fed cuts 3x by year-end.
3.  **P3:** ECB holds longer (relative to Fed).

This forms the **Prior Probability** of the thesis being correct. Let's assign a quantified prior belief for demonstration. Given the trader entered the position, they likely had high conviction. We'll set:
*   **P(Thesis True) = 70%** (Prior)

**New Evidence (E):**
*   **NFP:** +312k vs +175k consensus → **Strongly contradictory** to P1 (deceleration) and, by extension, P2 (Fed cuts).
*   **Wages:** +4.1% YoY → **Contradictory** to the disinflation narrative enabling Fed cuts (P2).
*   **GDPNow:** +3.4% Q2 → **Strongly contradictory** to P1.
*   **Fed Funds Futures:** Pricing 1.2 cuts vs. 3 cuts in thesis → **Directly contradictory** to P2.

**Assessing the Likelihood:**
This is the core of the Bayesian update. We ask: *"What is the probability of observing this evidence (E) if my thesis is TRUE, versus if it is FALSE?"*

*   **P(E | Thesis True):** The likelihood of seeing *such strong* employment, wage, growth, and pricing data *if* the US were decelerating and the Fed were poised to cut 3x is **very low**. This data is not a minor beat; it's a fundamental challenge. Let's assign **P(E|True) = 0.20** (a 20% chance this data could appear even if the thesis will ultimately prove correct—e.g., a temporary boom before a sharp slowdown).

*   **P(E | Thesis False):** The likelihood of seeing this data if the thesis is wrong (i.e., US resilience persists, Fed stays hawkish) is **very high**. Let's assign **P(E|False) = 0.90**.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**
`P(True | E) = [P(E|True) * P(True)] / [P(E|True) * P(True) + P(E|False) * P(False)]`

*   P(False) = 1 - P(True) = 0.30
*   Calculation:
    *   Numerator: 0.20 * 0.70 = 0.14
    *   Denominator: (0.20 * 0.70) + (0.90 * 0.30) = 0.14 + 0.27 = 0.41
    *   **Posterior Probability P(True|E) = 0.14 / 0.41 ≈ 0.34 or 34%.**

**Interpretation:** The new evidence has catastrophically degraded the thesis probability from a **70%** prior conviction to a **34%** posterior probability. The thesis is now more likely wrong than right. The most damaged pillar is P2 (Fed cuts), directly attacked by the market repricing. P1 (deceleration) is also severely challenged. P3 (ECB holds) remains intact but is now a secondary, less potent driver.

### **2. Thesis Invalidation vs. Temporary Noise**

This distinction is critical for deciding whether to *exit* or *adjust*.

*   **Temporary Noise:** Evidence that is backward-looking, subject to large revisions, statistically anomalous, or unlikely to change the central bank's *reaction function*. Example: A single CPI print that is hot due to seasonal adjustments, while forward-looking inflation expectations remain anchored. Noise may cause a *tactical market move* but not a *strategic shift* in the narrative supporting your thesis.

*   **Thesis Invalidation:** Evidence that directly undermines the *causal logic* of your thesis and is likely to alter the policy path of key decision-makers (like the Fed). It is often:
    1.  **Forward-Looking:** GDPNow is a forward estimate.
    2.  **Policy-Relevant:** NFP and wages are the Fed's dual mandate.
    3.  **Confirmed by Market Pricing:** Fed funds futures are the cleanest aggregation of informed expectations. A shift from 3 to 1.2 cuts is profound.
    4.  **High Statistical Significance:** The magnitude of the NFP beat is large.

**Judgment Call:** The new evidence is **strongly indicative of invalidation**, not noise. It directly attacks the core mechanism (Fed policy divergence) through the most important data channels. It has already changed the expected policy path.

### **3. Conditions to Cut vs. Maintain at Reduced Size**

The decision tree must be based on the **updated posterior probability (34%)** and the **risk management framework.**

#### **A. Conditions to CUT THE POSITION ENTIRELY (Exit):**
1.  **Direct Thesis Invalidation:** The trader concludes that Pillar 2 (Fed cuts 3x) is **dead**. If the new posterior probability is below a personal threshold (often 30-35%), the rational trade is to exit. The trader should ask: "Would I open a *new* short USD/long EUR position here at 1.0940 with this information?" If the answer is no, they must exit.
2.  **Breach of Key Risk Level:** The position is +120 pips. If the market reverses and trades below their **thesis-dependent stop-loss** (e.g., a close back below 1.0820, their entry), it signals the market agrees with the new data, not their aging thesis. Exit.
3.  **Loss of Causal Driver:** If the trader no longer believes the *primary driver* of the expected move (Fed-ECB divergence) is operative, the position becomes a passive, hope-based bet. This is a strict exit condition.

#### **B. Conditions to MAINTAIN WITH REDUCED SIZE:**
1.  **Thesis Damaged, Not Destroyed:** The trader judges that P1 and P2 are wounded but could revive with softer data in coming months. P3 (ECB hold) provides a partial, offsetting hedge. The thesis probability, while lowered, is still above their absolute exit threshold.
2.  **Position Sizing Adjustment:** The position size must be scaled down proportionally to the reduced conviction. A simple rule: if conviction dropped from 70% to 34% (~50% reduction), the position size should be **cut by at least 50%**. This aligns capital at risk with updated expectations.
3.  **Clear Catalysts for Re-assessment:** The trader identifies specific future data points (e.g., next CPI, ISM, ECB meetings) that could *re-validate* the original thesis and plans to reassess then. The position is now a smaller, tactical "watch and see" holding.

### **Recommended Action for This Trader**

Given the severity of the evidence:
1.  **Immediately Reduce Size:** The Bayesian update shows conviction more than halved. Reduce the position by **50-70%** immediately to align risk with the new 34% probability.
2.  **Move Stop-Loss to Breakeven (1.0820):** Lock in the remaining profit on the reduced position. This defines the risk-free remainder as a pure bet on the damaged thesis.
3.  **Define Exit Catalyst:** Announce that a **close above 1.1000** (a technical breakout affirming USD weakness is over) or **next CPI print >0.4% MoM** would drop the posterior probability below their personal 30% threshold, triggering a full exit of the remaining position.
4.  **Monitor for Re-validation:** Acknowledge that only a rapid deterioration in upcoming US data could resurrect the original thesis. The burden of proof has now flipped.

This process transforms the trader's response from an emotional reaction to a **disciplined, probabilistic adjustment**. They are not fleeing a loss (they are profitable); they are rationally re-allocating capital away from a thesis whose odds have objectively deteriorated. This is the essence of disciplined discretionary trading.
