    *   **Weakening Quadrant (Growth Under Pressure):**
        *   **XLK (Technology):** Heading into Lagging. This is the most bearish signal for growth. Tech is highly sensitive to liquidity (strong dollar = tighter financial conditions), long-duration earnings (hurt by higher discount rates), and global demand. Its trajectory indicates it's becoming a source of funds for other sectors.
    *   **Improving Quadrant (Cyclical Resilience & Beneficiaries):**
        *   **XLI (Industrials) & XLE (Energy):** Both showing strong clockwise momentum. This is the most intriguing part of the map.
            *   **XLE:** Benefits from a strong dollar (which often correlates with U.S. economic relative strength) and is a direct hedge against geopolitical or inflation concerns. It's moving against the broad "growth-to-value" rotation.
            *   **XLI:** Its strength here is likely *domestic and secular*. Industrials tied to U.S. infrastructure, onshoring, and defense are insulated from dollar strength. Their improvement suggests the market sees resilience in *specific* industrial capex, not broad global growth.

**Summary Implication:** The market is in a **transitional, risk-averse state**. It is rotating away from the previous leadership (Big Tech) and towards sectors that offer either 1) **defense** (Utilities), 2) **tangible assets/cash flows** (Energy), or 3) **exposure to insulated domestic cyclical themes** (Industrials). The strong DXY is the dominant macro force shaping these rotations.
