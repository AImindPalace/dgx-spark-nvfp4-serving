**3. White's Reality Check (WRC)**

White's Reality Check is a bootstrap method that accounts for the data-mining bias. The procedure: (1) generate B bootstrap samples (typically 1,000-10,000) by resampling the original data with replacement, (2) for each bootstrap sample, run the full 847-parameter optimization and record the best Sharpe ratio, (3) compare the actual in-sample Sharpe (1.84) to the distribution of best Sharpe ratios from the bootstrap samples, (4) the p-value is the proportion of bootstrap best-Sharpe values that exceed 1.84.

If the p-value is below 0.05, the strategy passes the WRC — meaning the observed Sharpe is unlikely to have arisen from pure data mining. If the p-value is above 0.05, the result is not statistically significant after correcting for the number of trials.
