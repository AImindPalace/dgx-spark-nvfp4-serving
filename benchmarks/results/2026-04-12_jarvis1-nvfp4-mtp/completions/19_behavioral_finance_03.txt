Tetlock's fox/hedgehog distinction is directly relevant: a hedgehog would say 'the economy is fine, I'm right to be 70% in equities.' A fox would ask: 'What if I'm wrong about the recession probability? What if the 25% is actually 40%? What if the recession is deeper than -25%?'
