This is a direct application of the Kelly Criterion framework to a real-world trading scenario, requiring both the mathematical calculation and the critical behavioral interpretation that Kelly's original formulation doesn't address.

**Kelly Calculation:**

The Kelly formula for a binary bet is: f* = (p × b - q) / b

Where:
- p = probability of winning = 0.58
- q = probability of losing = 1 - 0.58 = 0.42
- b = payoff ratio = average winner / average loser = 4.2 / 2.8 = 1.5

Plugging in: f* = (0.58 × 1.5 - 0.42) / 1.5 = (0.87 - 0.42) / 1.5 = 0.45 / 1.5 = 0.30

The full-Kelly fraction is 30% of the account per trade.

At $250,000, full-Kelly bet size = $250,000 × 0.30 = $75,000 per trade.

**Drawdown During the 9-Trade Loss Run:**

This is where the Kelly framework reveals its most dangerous property. Each loss reduces the account by 30% of the current balance. After 9 consecutive losses:

Account after 9 losses = $250,000 × (1 - 0.30)^9 = $250,000 × 0.70^9 = $250,000 × 0.04035 = $10,088

The trader would have lost 96% of their capital during the observed maximum consecutive-loss run.

**Why Full-Kelly Is Impractical for Live Discretionary Traders:**

Three fundamental problems make full-Kelly unusable in practice:

1. **Parameter estimation error:** The Kelly fraction is extremely sensitive to the input parameters. If the true win rate is 55% rather than 58% (a plausible estimation error), the Kelly fraction drops to: f* = (0.55 × 1.5 - 0.45) / 1.5 = (0.825 - 0.45) / 1.5 = 0.375 / 1.5 = 0.25, or 25%. But if the true payoff ratio is 1.3 rather than 1.5, Kelly drops to: f* = (0.58 × 1.3 - 0.42) / 1.3 = (0.754 - 0.42) / 1.3 = 0.334 / 1.3 = 0.257. The backtested parameters are estimates with standard errors, and Kelly amplifies estimation error into catastrophic overbetting. This is the overfitting problem that Aronson's framework addresses.

2. **Drawdown psychology:** A 96% drawdown is psychologically impossible for most traders to endure. Even if the trader has the mathematical conviction, the behavioral reality is that they will abandon the strategy during the drawdown. This is the gap between what the Kelly framework assumes (a rational agent who can endure any drawdown) and what real traders experience.

3. **Kelly's own warning:** Kelly himself recommended half-Kelly (f* / 2) as a practical compromise. Half-Kelly at 15% per trade produces a maximum drawdown of $250,000 × 0.85^9 = $250,000 × 0.2316 = $57,900, or a 76.8% drawdown — still severe but more survivable. Quarter-Kelly at 7.5% produces: $250,000 × 0.925^9 = $250,000 × 0.505 = $126,250, or a 49.5% drawdown — the most survivable of the three.

The practical recommendation is quarter-Kelly: $250,000 × 0.075 = $18,750 per trade. This preserves the mathematical edge while making the drawdown survivable.
