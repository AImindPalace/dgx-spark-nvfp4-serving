- **Gains are in the concave portion of the value function**: The trader is risk-averse in the domain of gains. Each additional dollar of profit feels less valuable than the previous one, so the marginal utility of holding a winner diminishes rapidly. Selling at 4.2 days locks in a gain that feels "safe" — the trader is avoiding the pain of seeing a winner turn into a loser.

- **Losses are in the convex portion of the value function**: The trader is risk-seeking in the domain of losses. The convex utility function means the trader is willing to take on increasing risk (holding longer) to avoid the certain pain of a realized loss.
