This is a multi-factor macro synthesis that requires integrating the yield curve, credit conditions, labor market, and inflation dynamics to determine the credit cycle phase. The framework here draws from the credit cycle model and the historical pattern that recessions follow yield curve re-steepening by 6–18 months.

**Credit Cycle Phase Assessment:**

The yield curve has fully un-inverted: from -108 bps to +22 bps. This is a significant 130 bps move that signals the market's expectation of future rate cuts — but the Fed's current stance (4.50% Fed Funds, core PCE at 2.8%) means those cuts are not imminent. This creates a divergence: the bond market is pricing easing while the Fed is constrained by sticky inflation. This divergence is itself a signal — the market is ahead of the Fed by approximately 6–12 months in its rate-cut expectations.

The ISM Manufacturing PMI at 48.3 is in contraction territory (below 50), and the JOLTS ratio falling from 2.0x to 1.1x over 18 months represents a 45% deterioration in labor market tightness. This is the mechanism through which credit tightening transmits to the real economy: higher rates → tighter credit conditions → reduced hiring → declining demand → recession. The JOLTS ratio is one of the most reliable leading indicators of employment-driven recessions.

**Sector Rotation Framework:**

Historically, the 6–18 months following yield curve re-steepening favor defensive sectors. The rotation pattern is: financials (which benefit from re-steepening) → consumer staples and utilities (defensive) → industrials (lagging, as PMI declines). With ISM at 48.3, we're in the early defensive rotation phase.

**Soft-Landing Conditions:**

The soft-landing thesis requires three things to hold simultaneously: (1) core PCE must decline below 2.5% within 6 months, (2) the JOLTS ratio must stabilize above 1.5x (indicating labor market resilience), and (3) the Fed must signal rate cuts within 3 months. If all three occur, the credit cycle could extend without a recession. If any one fails, the recession probability rises above 65%.
