**Full-Kelly Bet Size:** 30% of $250,000 = $75,000 per signal.

**Drawdown During 9-Consecutive-Loss Run:**

The geometric decay under full-Kelly is devastating:

| Loss # | Bankroll | Drawdown |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Start | $250,000 | 0% |
| 1 | $175,000 | 30% |
| 2 | $122,500 | 51% |

**Why Full-Kelly Is Impractical for Live Discretionary Trading:**

1. **Parameter Uncertainty:** The 58% win rate and 1.5 payoff ratio are estimates from 5 years of data. If the true win rate is 55% (within normal statistical variation), Kelly drops to approximately 22%. If the true payoff ratio is 1.3, Kelly drops to approximately 17%. 2. **Volatility of the Kelly Fraction:** A trader who re-estimates Kelly weekly based on the most recent 50 trades will experience extreme position size swings. The Kelly fraction itself becomes a source of additional volatility beyond the strategy's inherent variance.

Even a sound strategy will be abandoned during the drawdown, converting a temporary statistical event into a permanent failure. **Practical Recommendation — Fractional Kelly:**

Apply 25% Kelly: 7.5% of bankroll per signal = $18,750 per trade.

The 9-loss drawdown under 25% Kelly: approximately 50% ($125,000 remaining). This is painful but survivable. The trader can continue executing the strategy through the drawdown, preserving the statistical edge.

Additionally, implement a maximum position size cap of 5% of bankroll ($12,500) as a hard constraint, since the strategy fires 3–5 signals per week and the trader needs to maintain diversification across multiple simultaneous positions.

---
**Key Risk Identification:** The Kelly framework's primary failure mode is parameter uncertainty — the strategy's win rate and payoff ratio are estimates, not known quantities. Full-Kelly treats estimated parameters as truth, which systematically overestimates the optimal fraction. The 25% Kelly rule of thumb is specifically designed to address this uncertainty.
