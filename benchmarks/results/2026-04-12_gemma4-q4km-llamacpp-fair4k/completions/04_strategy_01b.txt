**Full-Kelly Calculation:** The Kelly formula is K% = W - [(1 - W) / R], where W = win rate, R = average winner / average loser. **The Case for Fractional Kelly:** Tharp's position sizing framework, which builds on the Kelly method, explicitly recommends fractional Kelly for live trading. The reasons are well-documented. First, the Kelly formula assumes you know your true win rate and R-multiple — but you only have estimates from backtests. If the true win rate is actually 52% instead of 58%, full-30% Kelly would be massively overbetting and could lead to rapid drawdown. Second, Tharp emphasizes that the Kelly formula is a maximum-growth-rate solution, not a risk-management tool. It maximizes expected geometric growth but ignores the path — you could experience a 50% drawdown on the way to that maximum growth. Third, the Kelly formula assumes independent, identically distributed trades — but real markets have regimes, correlations, and fat tails. Fourth, Tharp's R-multiple framework requires you to know your average winner and average loser precisely, but in live trading you only discover these after the fact.
