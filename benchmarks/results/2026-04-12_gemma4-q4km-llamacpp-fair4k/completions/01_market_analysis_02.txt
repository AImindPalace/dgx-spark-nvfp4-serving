The historical pattern is that recessions typically arrive during inversion, and the real economic damage manifests in the 6–18 months after the curve re-steepens, as credit tightening works through the economy. This means we are likely in the early phase of a credit cycle where the recession has already begun but its full impact hasn't yet been felt. The ISM PMI below 50 confirms manufacturing contraction, and the JOLTS ratio decline from 2.0x to 1.1x shows a significant loosening of labor market tightness — the economy is moving from a labor shortage to a labor surplus, which historically precedes unemployment increases.
