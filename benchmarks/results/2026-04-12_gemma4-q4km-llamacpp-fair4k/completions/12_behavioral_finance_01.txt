**Signal Identification — The Sentiment Composite:**
The data presents an extreme bullish consensus across four independent sentiment measures, all at or near historical extremes. The AAII at 58.3% bulls (2.5 standard deviations above mean) indicates retail optimism at levels not seen since 2018. The Investors Intelligence Bull/Bear ratio at 4.2x is a 6-year high. Put/call ratio at 0.72 is a 5-year low — meaning call buying dominates put buying at the options level. NAAIM cash at 3.2% means institutional managers are fully invested. Margin debt at 2.8% of market cap shows leveraged buying is elevated. This is not a single data point — it's a consensus across retail, institutional, options, and margin dimensions.

**Framework Application — Shiller and Marks on Sentiment Extremes:**
Shiller's work on irrational exuberance demonstrates that extreme bullish sentiment is a reliable predictor of subsequent underperformance — not because sentiment itself causes declines, but because it signals that the market has already priced in the best-case scenario. When 58.3% of retail investors are bullish, the marginal buyer is increasingly likely to be selling. Marks' market cycle framework adds the positioning dimension: at 3.2% NAAIM cash, there are no more buyers coming from institutional reallocation. At 2.8% margin debt, the next marginal buyer is increasingly leveraged. The forward return distribution in this sentiment regime historically skews toward the lower 25th percentile — mean forward 12-month returns in extreme-bullish sentiment regimes average approximately +4-6% but with a wide distribution including -15% to -25% outcomes.

**The EPS Growth and Fed Cutting Complication:**
The 12-month forward EPS consensus of +14% and the Fed cutting cycle are genuinely important counter-signals. Unlike pure sentiment extremes in 1999-2000 (where earnings growth was also +15-20%), the current market has a fundamental backdrop that could support continued gains. The Fed cutting cycle historically has a positive correlation with equity returns over 12-month horizons (+12-15% average in cutting environments). This means the contrarian signal is weaker than it would be in a pure sentiment extreme without fundamental support. The question is whether the sentiment extreme is a leading indicator of a correction or a lagging indicator of a healthy bull market.

**Contrarian Positioning Strategy:**
The correct approach is not to short the market but to reduce exposure and increase optionality. At NAAIM cash of 3.2%, you're already fully invested — the actionable signal is to begin trimming toward 15-20% cash allocation. The entry trigger for increasing cash is the first weekly close below the 50-day moving average on the S&P 500, which would signal the momentum regime is breaking. The target for increased cash is 20% of portfolio value. The stop on the contrarian thesis — the signal to abandon the view and re-enter — is the AAII falling below 45% bulls (returning to the normal range) while the S&P 500 makes a new 52-week high.

**Override Conditions:**
Override the contrarian signal if: (1) the Fed cuts by more than 75 basis points within 2 months, which would indicate a recessionary cutting cycle rather than a normal easing cycle — this changes the fundamental backdrop entirely; (2) the S&P 500 breaks above its all-time high by more than 5% on rising volume, which would indicate the sentiment extreme is being validated by price action rather than preceding a correction; or (3) the 12-month forward EPS consensus is revised upward above +18%, suggesting the fundamental case is strengthening faster than sentiment is deteriorating.
