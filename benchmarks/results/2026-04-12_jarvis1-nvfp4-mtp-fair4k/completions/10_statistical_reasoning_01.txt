**White's Reality Check (WRC) Procedure**

The analyst should apply White's Reality Check, a bootstrap method that adjusts p-values for multiple testing. The procedure:

1. **Permute the returns**: Randomly shuffle the daily SPY returns 1,000 times, destroying any serial correlation while preserving the distribution of returns.

2. **Re-optimize**: For each permutation, run the same 847-parameter optimization and record the best Sharpe ratio found.

3. **Build the null distribution**: The 1,000 best-Sharpe values from permuted data form the null distribution of "best Sharpe achievable by pure chance."

4. **Calculate the adjusted p-value**: Count how many permuted best-Sharpe values exceed the observed 1.84. Divide by 1,000.

If the adjusted p-value exceeds 0.05, the strategy's performance is not statistically distinguishable from noise. Given my estimate above that 1.84 is the expected maximum from noise, the p-value will likely be well above 0.05 — probably in the 0.30–0.50 range.

**Out-of-Sample Testing Requirements**

Before presenting to investors, the analyst must demonstrate out-of-sample performance. Minimum requirements:

- **Out-of-sample length**: At least 3 years of data not used in any optimization. If the 2010–2024 period was used for optimization, the strategy should be tested on 2025+ data (forward testing) or, if that's not available, the last 3 years of the 2010–2024 period should be held out entirely.

- **Walk-forward optimization**: Instead of optimizing on the full 2010–2024 period, use a rolling window approach. For example, optimize on a 5-year window and test on the following year, then roll forward by 1 year. This produces a series of out-of-sample Sharpe ratios that can be averaged.
