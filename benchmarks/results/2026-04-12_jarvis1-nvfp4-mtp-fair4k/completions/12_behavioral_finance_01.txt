**Framework Application — Shiller's Irrational Exuberance and Marks' Cycle Positioning:**

Shiller's core thesis is that investor psychology drives prices far from fundamental values, and that extreme sentiment readings are among the most reliable contrarian indicators. His work on the long-run equity risk premium shows that periods of extreme optimism are associated with below-average forward returns.

Marks' framework for cycle positioning emphasizes that the market's greatest danger is complacency — when everyone is bullish, the margin of safety is zero. His key insight: "The best time to buy is when others are selling, and the best time to sell is when others are buying."

**Historical Return Distribution in Extreme Sentiment Regimes:**

Research from multiple sources (AAII, Investors Intelligence, NAAIM) shows that when sentiment reaches 2+ standard deviations above the mean:
- 12-month forward S&P 500 returns average approximately -2% to +3% (versus the long-run average of ~10%)
- 24-month forward returns average approximately -5% to +5%
- The distribution is highly skewed: the median outcome is negative, but the right tail is fat — meaning you can be contrarian and still get crushed if a genuine earnings boom materializes

**The Contradiction — Earnings Growth and Monetary Policy:**

Here's where the analysis gets nuanced. The sentiment extremes are bearish, but the fundamental backdrop (14% EPS growth, Fed cutting) is genuinely supportive. This creates a genuine analytical tension:

1. If the 14% EPS growth is real and sustainable, the market may be correctly priced despite extreme sentiment — the contrarian signal is a false positive
2. If the 14% EPS growth is a consensus estimate that will disappoint (as consensus estimates frequently do at cycle peaks), the sentiment extremes are correctly warning of a downturn
3. The Fed cutting cycle provides a floor under risk assets, which historically reduces the severity of drawdowns even from elevated sentiment readings

I would override the contrarian bearish signal if:
